## The starting point

A narrow infill parcel between two existing buildings in central Olten. The owner
had planning intentions but no answer to the only question a bank cares about:
what can be built here, and will it sell fast enough to be financed?

## The chain

**Esad** ran the feasibility study — testing massing, daylight and yield against
the zoning envelope — and developed the design through to permit-ready plans and
sections. The result was a compact 18-apartment building that used the awkward
plot to its advantage.

**Dionis** set the pricing strategy and built the sales dossier, then pre-sold the
apartments off-plan. With renders, a brochure and a project microsite produced by
**CloudOnPoint**, buyers could see and reserve homes that did not yet exist.

## The outcome

The building reached **61% pre-sold within four months** — comfortably past the
quota the bank required to release construction credit, before ground was broken.
Every remaining unit sold through by completion.
